The deadliest eruption ever was Mount Tambora (1815, Indonesia), killing an estimated 60,000 people. It ejected so much ash into the atmosphere that global temperatures dropped, causing the 1816 "Year Without a Summer," leading to widespread crop failures. In New England, there was snow in June. In Europe, food shortages led to riots. Also, this weird summer indoors led Mary Shelley to write Frankenstein.
